Website Evaluations

Apple

Apple’s official website demonstrates professional web design. It uses clean layout, white space, and visual hierarchy to emphasize products.

  • Contrast: Clear black text on white, strong product photos stand out.
  • Repetition: Fonts, colors, and spacing are consistent across all pages.
  • Alignment: Everything is neatly grid-based, well aligned, and orderly.
  • Proximity: Related information is grouped, such as product features and purchase links.

Assessment: Apple’s site strongly follows all CRAP principles, making it a model for effective web design.

Ling’s Cars

Ling’s Cars is infamous for chaotic, distracting design. It overwhelms visitors with clashing colors, animations, and inconsistent elements.

  • Contrast: Too many conflicting colors create visual clutter.
  • Repetition: Little consistency; styles change constantly.
  • Alignment: Content feels scattered with poor alignment.
  • Proximity: Unrelated items are crammed together, making it confusing.

Assessment: This site ignores CRAP principles, showing why these guidelines matter. It’s memorable, but for the wrong reasons.
